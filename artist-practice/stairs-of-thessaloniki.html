Stairs of Thessaloniki Project

Here is the content of your Letter of Motivation. You can write your text directly here or copy and paste from a document.

Stairs of Thessaloniki is an ongoing archival and visual research project that documents gravestone fragments from the old Jewish cemetery of Thessaloniki, demolished in the mid-20th century after World War II. These gravestones—once part of one of the largest Sephardic cemeteries in Europe—were repurposed as building material across the city, most visibly as steps in the old town. There is no public registry of the fragments. Traces remain: Hebrew inscriptions embedded in stairways, cornerstones of churches, and municipal structures.

The project considers Thessaloniki as a palimpsest of cultural erasure and revision. Once a thriving center of Jewish intellectual and mercantile life in the 19th century, the city was known as a cosmopolitan center where Ladino, Greek, Turkish, and French were spoken in the streets. Following dramatic political shifts to the formation of the modern Greek state building project—this pluralism was gradually replaced by cultural and religious homogeneity. The cemetery’s destruction, and the reuse of its stones, mark a material layer of this nation-building process, along with the deportation of the city’s Jewish population to Nazi concentration camps in occupied Poland.

By locating and mapping these scattered fragments, the project attempts to reconstruct a lost topography. It attempts to account for an architectures of loss. In doing so, it also shows modern Thessaloniki as a stage in a multi-layered history- and uncovers the many iterations in the city's past.
