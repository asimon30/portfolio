Open Source Aesthetics

Open Source Aesthetics is an ongoing visual research project developed collaboratively by artists, curators, humanitarian aid workers, and human rights monitoring researchers. Drawing on methods of open source investigation, the project examines the aesthetic residue of human rights monitoring—foregrounding the visual byproducts of documentation and verification efforts.

By presenting materials used in human rights investigations through an artistic lens, the project interrogates the role of aesthetic imagery in both advocacy and research. It invites reflection on how such imagery functions—not only as evidence, but also as a constructed and mediated form— prompting critical inquiry into its context, and questioning what is lost when these images are presented alienated from their context.

The project aims to provoke dialogue at the intersection of image and source, aesthetics and politics—complicating notions of truth, visibility, and representation in contemporary human rights methods.
