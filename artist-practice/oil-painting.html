Series: Dew Mouth

Dew Mouth is a series of oil paintings, partially exhibited at Bard College Berlin's Open Studios in the spring of 2022. The project emerged from visual research into the phenomenon of "Dew Mouth"—a specific form of tooth decay endemic to the Appalachian region of the southeastern United States. In mountainous areas with limited access to water infrastructure, residents often rely on Mountain Dew, a type of soda, in place of clean drinking water.

What began as a study of localized decay evolved into a broader exploration of symbolic deterioration within the United States. The paintings depict teeth visibly marked by this decay, distorted and combined with other symbols of the mythic American narrative: engines, industrial machinery, horses, and the aesthetics of power. These teeth serve as microcosmic evidence of larger sociopolitical breakdowns. They are corporeal manifestations of a broader collapse—reflecting the erosion of American political exceptionalism through the body.
